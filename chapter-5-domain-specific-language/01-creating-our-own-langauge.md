At the time of writing, we have seen tens of not hundereds of different blockchains and supporting layers, such as layer 2’s and layer 0s.  Each one seems to be claiming the same things.  Faster tranascations or more bandwidth in the form of transactions per second “TPS”, or lower fees.

The problem with the lower fees is that just like opening a new toll road, the advantage is that there is few cars on the road making it somewhat attractive for consumers.  As the congestion builds up, so do the fees.

The supply and demand model finds the equilibrium, between what the consumer is willing to spend for that convenience.

As more users adopt or drivers take that road…

Ethereum developers have created smart contract programming languages, that allows users to develop in a fimilar syntax, and compile down to the EVM OP codes or instructions that the EVM understands.

Solidity, is a c style language that has a syntax that JavaScript developers will find familiar.  Vyper is a language that Python developers will find familiar, but is the least popular out of the two.
